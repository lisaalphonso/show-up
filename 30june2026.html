Habit completion
Diabetes runs in your family. Cutting sugar is not about willpower — it's about retraining your taste buds and blood sugar response. It takes about 2 weeks to stop craving it.
Week 1 — Cut the obvious
1
Remove sweets, biscuits, mithai from arm's reach — out of sight works
2
Cut chai/coffee sugar by half first, then fully over 7 days
3
When cravings hit: drink water, eat fruit, or walk 5 minutes
4
Read labels — ketchup, packaged atta, juices all hide sugar
Week 2 — Stabilise blood sugar
5
Never eat carbs alone — always pair with protein or fat (dal + roti, not just roti)
6
Eat within 1 hour of waking — skipping breakfast spikes sugar later
7
Swap maida for jowar, bajra, or multigrain — one swap a day is enough
Week 3+ — Make it permanent
8
Cook dal, sabzi, curd-based meals — these are your best friends
9
Whole fruit is fine. Mango in season is okay, once a day. No juice.
10
At social occasions: eat a small portion, not zero — zero leads to bingeing

Cutting 80% of your sugar intake is a massive win for someone with a family diabetes history. You don't need to be perfect.

Confidence rebuild — the one rule

You were never not yourself. You've just been uncomfortable for a bit. Confidence comes back through action, not motivation. Every walk completed, every 10 pages read, every sugar skipped — you're telling yourself you can be trusted. Do that enough and the feeling follows.

Systems that actually work
1
One accountability person
Send them your step count every evening. Just the number. 10 seconds. It works.
2
The 2-day rule
You can miss 1 day but never 2 in a row. One miss is human. Two is a pattern.
3
Don't break the chain
Mark each day on the tracker. Seeing the streak makes you not want to ruin it.
4
Weekly check-in
Every Sunday, 5 minutes: what worked, what didn't, what changes next week.
5
Identity shift
Stop saying "I'm trying to lose weight." Say "I'm someone who walks every day."
